Why Sticking to a Bedtime Routine Is Harder with ADHD

Inconsistent Routines

Routines often fall apart—especially when they feel boring or easy to forget. Without structure, steps get skipped.

Off-Schedule Body Clock

ADHD often comes with a delayed sense of timing. Typical bedtime hours might not feel natural, and without external reminders, tasks tend to get pushed later and later.

Revenge Bedtime Procrastination

Nighttime feels like the only free time. That freedom leads to putting off bedtime tasks—even when rest is needed.

Mental Overdrive at Night

As the day winds down, the ADHD brain can become more active—filled with thoughts, ideas, and distractions. This heightened mental activity makes it difficult to focus on simple nighttime tasks.
